---
title: "Getting Started with the Inbox Agent"
description: "Start using the Inbox Agent in 5 minutes"
---

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Outlook & Pick an Email">
    Open your **Outlook inbox** and select any incoming email from a client. The Inbox Agent is designed to work where you already are — directly inside Outlook.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Launch the Inbox Agent">
    Find the Ravical icon in the **right-hand sidebar**. Click it to open the Inbox Agent panel.

    💡 **Don’t see it?**

    - Go to **Apps** in Outlook
    - Search for **Ravical**
    - Pin it so it stays open while you browse emails
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review & Refine the Draft">
    The Inbox Agent will generate a **draft reply** based on:

    - The email thread
    - Relevant data from internal tools
    - Your tone of voice

    You can then:

    - **Edit** the draft
    - Ask for changes (e.g., “maak het korter”, “schrijf formeler”)
    - **Add details** the agent missed

    <Tip>
      Use clear instructions at the bottom of the panel to tailor the response. The more you guide the Agent, the more it learns.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Use and Send">
    Happy with the result?

    Click **“Concept gebruiken”** to insert the draft reply into your email window, then press send.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### **🔹 Tips for First-Time Use**

- 📌 **Pin the sidebar** so it stays open while you switch emails
- 📝 **Instruct the agent** to personalise replies:

  > “Gebruik een opsomming”
  >
  > “Voeg een uitleg toe in eenvoudige taal”
  >
  > “Gebruik de standaardafsluiting van het kantoor”

### **🔹 What’s Happening Behind the Scenes?**

- The Agent performs **smart triage**: Only client conversations get replies.
- It pulls info from tools like CRM, SharePoint, and calendars.
- It learns from your last replies to that client (or falls back to general style).
- You **always stay in control** – nothing is sent without your OK.

🧠 _Imagine the Inbox Agent as a highly-trained assistant who knows your tools, mimics your voice, and is always ready with a first draft._
